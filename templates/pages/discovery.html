News

  • The memory uncovers the secret of its "fuel"

    today - Researchers from the Brain Mind Institute at EPFL and the Mount Sinai School of Medicine in New York have highlighted the crucial role played by a by-product of glucose – lactate – in the formation of long-term memory. This could lead to new therapeutic perspectives for some neurological disorders.

  • Eight professors appointed at EPFL

    04.03.11 - During the session of March 2nd and 3rd 2011, the ETH Board announced three appointments concerning EPFL, among which were three new scientific personalities who will work at the Ecublens campus.

  • Getting your sight back – thanks to an artificial retina

    03.03.11 - A new prosthetic retina enables those patients blinded by the disease "retinitis pigmentosa" to regain some form of vision. The company Second Sight, whose European branch is hosted by EPFL’s Science Park, has just received authorization to commercialize the product in Europe.
