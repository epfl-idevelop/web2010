Tips and examples for using the Inktomi search engine

It is easy to search Inktomi Search. Just enter a few words or phrases. Try to use precise words that are probably only in the documents you are searching for. The quality of your results depends on the number of words. Here are some examples :

Start the search by entering some words or phrases

Inktomi Search will retrieve the documents including these words and phrases as many times as possible and will display the documents which best match your request at the beginning. You will not miss an interesting document because it does not include one of the words in your query. Inktomi Search displays the documents even if they do not include all the search terms.

Enclose the phrases in quotes and separate them with commas

A phrase is entered by using quotes and only corresponds to the adjacent words. Separate multiple phrases or proper names with a comma.

Use UPPER CASE LETTERS to indicate an exact match.

If search terms are in lower case letters, the search will be made for lower case and upper case, if they are in upper case letters the search will only be for upper case. For example, a search for "suivant" will obtain Suivant, suivant and SuiVANT, while a search for SuiVANT will only obtain SuiVANT.

For more information, see the Inktomi help page.
